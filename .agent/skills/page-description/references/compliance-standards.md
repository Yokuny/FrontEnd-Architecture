# Maritime Compliance Standards & Regulations

## IMO (International Maritime Organization) Key Conventions

### SOLAS (Safety of Life at Sea)
The most important treaty covering ship safety, including construction standards, equipment, and operations.

**Key Chapters:**
- Chapter I: General provisions
- Chapter II-1: Construction (subdivision, stability, machinery, electrical)
- Chapter II-2: Fire protection, detection, and extinction
- Chapter III: Life-saving appliances
- Chapter IV: Radio communications (GMDSS)
- Chapter V: Safety of navigation
- Chapter VI: Carriage of cargoes
- Chapter VII: Carriage of dangerous goods
- Chapter IX: Management for safe ship operations (ISM Code)
- Chapter XI: Special measures to enhance maritime safety and security (ISPS)
- Chapter XII: Additional safety measures for bulk carriers

### MARPOL (Marine Pollution Prevention)

**Annex I - Oil Pollution:**
- Discharge limits: <15 ppm outside special areas
- Oil Record Book (ORB) mandatory
- IOPP Certificate required
- SOPEP (Shipboard Oil Pollution Emergency Plan)

**Annex II - Noxious Liquid Substances:**
- Cargo Record Book
- Pre-wash requirements
- Discharge standards by category (X, Y, Z, OS)

**Annex III - Packaged Harmful Substances:**
- IMDG Code compliance
- Packaging and labeling requirements

**Annex IV - Sewage:**
- Treatment plant or holding tank
- Discharge >3 nm from land (treated) or >12 nm (untreated)

**Annex V - Garbage:**
- Garbage Management Plan
- Garbage Record Book
- Discharge restrictions by type and distance
- Plastic discharge prohibited

**Annex VI - Air Pollution:**
- SOx limits: 0.5% global, 0.1% ECA
- NOx Tier III in ECAs
- ODS (Ozone Depleting Substances) prohibition
- VOC controls for tankers
- EEXI, CII, and SEEMP requirements

### ISM Code (International Safety Management)

**Requirements:**
- Safety and Environmental Protection Policy
- Company responsibilities and authority
- Designated Person Ashore (DPA)
- Master's responsibility and authority
- Resources and personnel
- Development of plans for shipboard operations
- Emergency preparedness
- Reports and analysis of non-conformities
- Maintenance of ship and equipment
- Documentation (SMS manual)
- Company verification, review, and evaluation
- Certification and periodic verification

**Documentation:**
- Document of Compliance (DOC) - Company level
- Safety Management Certificate (SMC) - Ship level
- Non-conformity reports (NCR)
- Internal audit reports
- Management review records

### ISPS Code (International Ship and Port Facility Security)

**Ship Security Levels:**
- Level 1: Normal (minimum protective security)
- Level 2: Heightened (additional protective security)
- Level 3: Exceptional (further specific protective security)

**Requirements:**
- Ship Security Plan (SSP)
- Ship Security Officer (SSO)
- Company Security Officer (CSO)
- Ship Security Alert System (SSAS)
- Security assessments and drills
- International Ship Security Certificate (ISSC)

### STCW (Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping)

**Core Requirements:**
- Minimum age: 16 years
- Medical fitness standards
- Basic safety training (all seafarers)
- Watchkeeping standards and rest hours
- Certification requirements by rank
- Endorsement by flag state
- Revalidation every 5 years

**Training Certificates:**
- Basic Safety Training (BST)
- Proficiency in Survival Craft (PSC)
- Advanced Fire Fighting (AFF)
- Medical First Aid / Medical Care
- Ship Security Officer (SSO)
- Designated Security Duties (DSD)
- Bridge Resource Management (BRM)
- Engine Resource Management (ERM)

## Environmental Performance & Reporting

### EEOI (Energy Efficiency Operational Indicator)

**Calculation:**
```
EEOI = (Fuel Consumed × Fuel Carbon Factor) / (Cargo Carried × Distance)
Units: grams CO₂ / tonne-nautical mile
```

**Best Practices:**
- Voyage-by-voyage calculation
- Fleet benchmarking
- Trend analysis
- Operational optimization

### CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator)

**Rating System:**
- A: Major superior (top 20%)
- B: Minor superior (20-30%)
- C: Moderate (30-70%)
- D: Minor inferior (70-80%)
- E: Inferior (bottom 20%)

**Calculation:**
```
CII = (Annual CO₂ Emissions) / (Capacity × Distance)
Units: grams CO₂ / capacity-nautical mile
```

**Compliance:**
- Annual rating mandatory from 2023
- D or E rating for 3 consecutive years triggers action plan
- Improvement trajectory: 2% reduction per year 2023-2026

### EEXI (Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index)

**Calculation:**
```
EEXI = (Power × SFOC × CF) / (Capacity × Speed)
```

**Compliance:**
- One-time certification (similar to EEDI for new ships)
- Must meet required EEXI based on ship type and size
- Valid until ship modification
- Technical measures: engine power limitation, energy-saving devices

### Data Collection & Reporting

**DCS (Data Collection System) - IMO:**
- Fuel oil consumption by fuel type
- Distance traveled
- Hours underway
- Cargo carried
- Transport work
- Annual reporting to flag state

**MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, Verification) - EU:**
- CO₂ emissions per voyage
- Fuel consumption
- Cargo carried
- Distance traveled
- Time at sea
- Verification by accredited body
- Reporting to EU for voyages to/from/within EU ports

**EU ETS (Emissions Trading System):**
- Phase-in: 40% (2024), 70% (2025), 100% (2026)
- Applies to ships >5000 GT
- Carbon allowances required
- 50% of emissions for voyages to/from EU
- 100% for voyages within EU
- Penalties for non-compliance

## Class Society Requirements

### Classification Surveys

**Annual Survey:**
- Hull inspection (external)
- Safety equipment
- Machinery examination
- Class certificate renewal

**Intermediate Survey:**
- Hull inspection (internal spaces)
- Machinery overhaul
- Bottom inspection (docking or in-water)
- Mid-cycle between special surveys

**Special Survey (every 5 years):**
- Complete hull examination
- Thickness measurements
- Structural assessment
- All machinery overhaul
- Stability and damage stability

**Continuous Survey:**
- Alternative to special survey
- Annual surveys cover 20% of requirements
- Completed over 5-year cycle

### Common Class Notations

**Hull:**
- ESP (Enhanced Survey Programme) - bulk carriers/tankers
- CSR (Common Structural Rules)
- SOLAS - passenger vessel standards

**Machinery:**
- AUT - unmanned machinery space
- DPS - dynamic positioning system
- IWS - integrated automation system

**Environmental:**
- Clean Design - environmental standards
- Green Passport - IHM (Inventory of Hazardous Materials)
- CLEAN - oil tanker protection

**Special Purpose:**
- ICE - ice-strengthened hull
- HELDK - helicopter deck
- OFFSHORE - offshore support operations

### Inspection Targeting

**Ship Risk Profile:**
- Low Risk Ship (LRS): Inspected every 36 months
- Standard Risk Ship (SRS): Inspected every 24 months
- High Risk Ship (HRS): Inspected every 5-6 months

**Risk Factors:**
- Flag state performance
- Recognized organization (Class society)
- Company performance
- Ship type and age
- Deficiency history

### Common Deficiency Categories
1. Safety of navigation (charts, GPS, radar)
2. Life-saving appliances (lifeboats, life jackets)
3. Fire safety (extinguishers, detection, drills)
4. Emergency systems (alarms, procedures)
5. Certificates and documentation
6. Pollution prevention (MARPOL compliance)
7. Working and living conditions (MLC)
8. Crew certification (STCW)
9. ISM implementation

**Detention Criteria:**
- Serious deficiencies (typically 5+ grounds)
- Critical safety issues
- Pollution risk
- Previous detention or deficiency record
- Over-age vessels with poor maintenance

### Flag State Offshore Codes

### Vetting Programs

**SIRE (Ship Inspection Report Programme):**
- Oil Companies International Marine Forum (OCIMF)
- Tanker vetting
- VIQ (Vessel Inspection Questionnaire) 7
- Valid 6 months
- Terminal operator access

**CDI (Chemical Distribution Institute):**
- Chemical tanker vetting
- Inspections valid 6 months
- Industry-wide recognition

**OVID (Offshore Vessel Inspection Database):**
- Offshore vessel vetting
- Managed by OCIMF
- Multiple operators use inspections

**OVMSA (Offshore Vessel Management Self-Assessment):**
- Self-assessment tool
- Management system evaluation
- Best practices benchmarking

### DP (Dynamic Positioning) Standards
- IMO MSC/Circ.645 - Guidelines for DP systems
- DP Class 1: Loss of position after single failure
- DP Class 2: No loss of position after single failure
- DP Class 3: No loss of position after fire or flood
- Annual DP trials
- DP operations manual
- FMEA (Failure Mode Effects Analysis)

## MLC 2006 (Maritime Labour Convention)

### Five Titles

**Title 1: Minimum Requirements for Seafarers**
- Minimum age (16, 18 for certain work)
- Medical certification
- Training and qualifications
- Recruitment and placement

**Title 2: Conditions of Employment**
- Seafarer employment agreements
- Wages
- Working hours and rest periods
- Leave entitlement
- Repatriation

**Title 3: Accommodation, Recreation, Food & Catering**
- Accommodation standards
- Recreational facilities
- Food and catering quality

**Title 4: Health Protection, Medical Care, Welfare & Social Security**
- Medical care onboard and ashore
- Health and safety protection
- Access to shore-based welfare facilities
- Social security protection

**Title 5: Compliance and Enforcement**
- Flag state responsibilities
- Port state control
- Labor-supplying responsibilities
- Complaints and dispute resolution

**Documentation:**
- Maritime Labour Certificate (MLC)
- Declaration of Maritime Labour Compliance (DMLC) Parts I & II
- Seafarer Employment Agreement (SEA)

## Ballast Water Management

### IMO BWM Convention

**Standards:**
- Regulation D-1: Ballast exchange (mid-ocean)
- Regulation D-2: Performance standard (treatment)
  - <10 viable organisms per m³ (≥50 μm)
  - <10 viable organisms per ml (10-50 μm)
  - Indicator microbes limits

**Requirements:**
- Ballast Water Management Plan
- Ballast Water Record Book
- International BWM Certificate
- BWMS type approval (USCG or IMO)
- Commissioning test
- Annual survey

### Treatment Technologies
- UV treatment
- Electrolytic (chlorination)
- Ozone treatment
- Filtration + UV
- Heat treatment (TBT alternative)

**Compliance Deadlines:**
- Existing ships: By IOPP renewal (latest 2024)
- New builds: From construction
